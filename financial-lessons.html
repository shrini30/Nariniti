The 50-30-20 Rule

A simple budgeting method that divides your income into three clear categories for better money management.

How it works:

  • 50% for Needs: Rent, groceries, utilities, loan EMIs
  • 30% for Wants: Entertainment, dining out, shopping
  • 20% for Savings: Emergency fund, investments, debt repayment

Example: If you earn ₹50,000/month → ₹25,000 (needs), ₹15,000 (wants), ₹10,000 (savings)

2

Compound Interest Magic

Money earns interest, and that interest earns more interest. Start saving early for exponential growth.

The Power of Compound Interest:

When you invest ₹1,000 at 10% annual interest:

  • Year 1: ₹1,100 (₹100 interest)
  • Year 2: ₹1,210 (₹110 interest)
  • Year 10: ₹2,594
  • Year 20: ₹6,727

Key: Time is your biggest asset. Start early, even with small amounts!

3

Emergency Fund First

Always save 3-6 months' worth of expenses in a separate emergency fund for unexpected situations.

Why Emergency Fund is Critical:

  • Protects you from job loss or income reduction
  • Covers sudden medical emergencies
  • Prevents you from taking high-interest loans
  • Gives peace of mind and financial security

How much: If your monthly expenses are ₹30,000, save ₹90,000-₹1,80,000

Where to keep: High-yield savings account or liquid funds

4

Needs vs. Wants

Distinguish between things you need (essentials) vs. want (luxuries) to control impulsive spending.

Clear Examples:

🟢 NEEDS:
  • Rent/EMI
  • Groceries
  • Utilities
  • Transportation
  • Basic clothing
  • Health insurance
🔴 WANTS:
  • Designer clothes
  • Latest gadgets
  • Dining out frequently
  • Premium subscriptions
  • Luxury items
  • Expensive hobbies

Tip: Before buying, ask "Do I need this or just want it?"

5

Pay Yourself First

Treat saving like a bill you must pay every month. Set aside a fixed % of income before spending anything else.

How to Pay Yourself First:

  1. Set up automatic transfers: Move savings to separate account immediately after salary
  2. Start with 10-20%: Even ₹2,000 from ₹20,000 salary
  3. Increase gradually: Raise percentage with pay hikes
  4. Make it non-negotiable: Treat it like rent or EMI

Example: Salary day → Transfer ₹5,000 to savings → Then spend the remaining ₹45,000

6

Good Debt vs. Bad Debt

Understand the difference between debt that builds assets versus debt that drains your wealth.

✅ GOOD DEBT:
  • Home Loan: Builds property asset
  • Education Loan: Increases earning potential
  • Business Loan: Can generate income

Lower interest rates, tax benefits, builds wealth

❌ BAD DEBT:
  • Credit Card Debt: High interest (24-48%)
  • Personal Loans for wants: Expensive EMIs
  • Consumer EMIs: Phones, gadgets depreciate

High interest rates, no asset creation

Rule: Avoid bad debt, use good debt strategically

7

Start Investing Early

You don't need a lot to start. Even ₹500/month in mutual funds via SIP can grow massively over time.

Simple Investment Options:

  • SIP in Mutual Funds: Start with ₹500-1,000/month
  • PPF: ₹500-1.5 lakh/year, 15-year lock-in
  • ELSS: Tax-saving mutual funds
  • Gold ETF: Digital gold investment

Example: ₹2,000/month SIP for 20 years at 12% returns = ₹19.8 lakhs

Key: Start small, stay consistent, increase with income

8

Know Your Credit Score

Your credit score affects loan approvals and interest rates. Maintain a healthy score for better financial opportunities.

Credit Score Ranges:

  • 750-900: Excellent (best loan rates)
  • 650-749: Good (decent rates)
  • 550-649: Fair (higher rates)
  • Below 550: Poor (loan rejection risk)

How to Improve:

  • Pay all bills on time (35% of score)
  • Keep credit card usage under 30%
  • Don't close old credit cards
  • Check your credit report annually
  • Avoid multiple loan applications
9

Track Every Rupee

Use apps or notebooks to track daily spending. Awareness helps reduce leaks and builds mindful habits.

Expense Tracking Methods:

  • Mobile Apps: Walnut, ET Money, Money View
  • Spreadsheets: Simple Excel/Google Sheets
  • Notebooks: Write down daily expenses
  • Bank Statements: Review monthly spending

Categories to Track:

  • Food & Dining
  • Transportation
  • Entertainment
  • Shopping
  • Bills & Utilities
  • Miscellaneous

Tip: Review weekly to identify spending patterns

10

Insurance is Protection, Not Investment

Term insurance covers your life; health insurance covers medical bills. Don't mix insurance with investment.

Types of Essential Insurance:

  • Term Life Insurance: High coverage, low premium
  • Health Insurance: Medical expense coverage
  • Vehicle Insurance: Mandatory by law

Avoid These Insurance Mistakes:

  • ❌ ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plans) - poor returns
  • ❌ Endowment policies - low returns
  • ❌ Money-back policies - inflation killer

Golden Rule: Term insurance for protection + Mutual funds for investment = Better returns

Coverage: Life insurance = 10-12x annual income
